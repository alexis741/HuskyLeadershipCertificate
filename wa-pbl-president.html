OVERVIEW

A key learning experience during my undergraduate education was being a part of Phi Beta Lambda (PBL). PBL is the collegiate division of Future Business Leaders of America-Phi Beta Lambda (FBLA-PBL), which inspires and prepares students to become community-minded business leaders in a global society through relevant career preparation and leadership experiences.

My recent role as the Washington State President insisted responsibilities similarly like a startup: finding sponsors, increasing membership, promoting the organization across the Pacific Northwest, and organizing conferences. This experience was valuable to me because it taught me about professional development, leadership, and personal values.

PERSONAL IMPORTANCE OF WORK

I've been a FBLA-PBL member since 2011. In high school, FBLA has introduced me to real-world experiences like community service, networking, and parliamentary procedure; at the UW, PBL has toned my soft skills to further prepare my professional aspirations.

As State President, I learned the value of time management, event planning, and professionalism when it came to scheduling my academic quarters. Before this role, I served (and still currently serving) as Local Chapter President, where I re-chartered and led the UW chapter with several activities and committees throughout the year. This [local] leadership experience has led me to run for state office at the 2016 Washington PBL State Leadership Conference, and again in 2017 for re-election. These past two years has given me the ability to impact thousands of members, advisers, volunteers, and professionals in the largest and oldest student business association in the world.

TASKS ACCOMPLISHED

  • Scheduled chapter appearances
  • Presided the state association at business meetings
  • Hosted several events for the [local and state] chapter
  • Designed, launched, and operated the local and state PBL websites: sites.uw.edu/fblapbl and wapbl.org
  • Re-structured the organization's branding, marketing, and public outreach strategies
  • Recruited new members at live events
  • Led student chapter leadership workshops at conferences
  • Organized tours for campus visitors, including an Iowa secondary chapter of Business Professionals of America (BPA)
  • Trained members for several state and national case competitions
  • Judged FBLA (high school division of FBLA-PBL) statewide case competitions for 3+ years.

LEADERSHIP COMPETENCIES

  • Confidence: When I first joined FBLA-PBL in high school, I used to have the fear of public speaking. After years of talking and leading a variety of teams and chapters, I have developed enough confidence to speak to any sized audience without feeling a bit of stage fright. Some of my big-audience experiences including once running for a national position at the PBL National Leadership Conference, where I introduced my candidacy to an audience of 2,000 people that come far and wide from all over the nation. Recently, I held the 2018 State Leadership Conference in Centralia, WA, and biggest factor in the conference was my public speaking abilities during the opening and closing sessions.​
​
  • Organization: My organizational duties entailed as State President at during my term included organizing the State Leadership Conference, training new chapter and state officers in the Washington State Association, and implementing new programs that encourages membership development and community engagement. These activities have re-structured the state organization to where it is today: branding and marketing, additional collegiate chapters, internal documentation and leadership, and a competitive edge to statewide case competitions.

  • Self-Development: As a member for 7+ years, I thank the assoication for providing me the resources to develop as a leader, a community member, and as a human being. Up to this date, I have gained the abilities to market myself into internships and other professional experiences with resume and LinkedIn preparation, dressing for success, and confidence when networking with industry leaders when attending conferences throughout the year. My love for this organization has made create wonderful experiences with others, as well as inspiring thousands of student members in FBLA-PBL.
​​
  • Service: I volunteer at WA FBLA's State Business Leadership Conference every year since 2016 as a event judge. I've been to SBLC as an FBLA competitor in 2013-2015, but once I joined PBL, I was eligible to judge the secondary (high school) division's statewide events that ranged in certain events by skill and grade level. I judged in the following events: Business Ethics, Mock Interview, Future Business Leader, and Parliamentary Procedure. As a alumnus to the secondary division, I knew how competitive these events work, and that each student works hard to learn the necessary academic business concepts while gaining soft skills to acquire professional qualities in the real world. It was an exciting opportunity to give back to the state secondary division where I used to be a member of years ago. Plus, it was intriguing how I impacted scoring the performances of the each competitor at these conferences.
 
  • Facilitation: At my second term during my state presidency, we gained two members of the State Officer Team. The sudden transition of having 0 to 2 additional members of a small-but-mighty state collegiate division is a huge difference to the community. This motivated me to take the initiative of conducting team trainings for incoming officers, organizational outreach strategies, and preparation of state and national conferences. In addition, we became more well-known in Washington state through volunteering and networking with other divisions of the organization: FBLA and the FBLA-PBL Professional Division. It connects the entire organization as a whole, and later attracted other colleges to start a collegiate chapter. I expanded over 4 new collegiate chapters since my membership in PBL began when during my first academic quarter at the University of Washington.
 
  • Others' Contributions: Upon my two-year presidency, I tend to carry most responsibilities on myself because it allowed me to be creative with these projects. However, I sometimes take on too much and asked for help. Fortunately, my fellow officers gladly helped me with organizational tasks. Examples of contributions include: the annual calendar of events, the State Leadership Conference, organizational marketing and branding, retention of state membership, and the recruitment of chapters. Without the assistance of others, I would have most likely crashed and burned.

  • Receiving Feedback: My ideas and contributions for this organization do not just come from myself. I always ask for feedback from others because it'll help me analyze what worked and didn't worked. Even when I implemented new state programs and committees, some of them did not follow through as expected, resulting in some failed initiatives. It's difficult for leaders like myself to admit failures, but the feedback I received was so useful, it'd made me consider alternative strategies for the organization's annual program of work. Don't ignore the constant criticism, because sometimes it'll help your cause and its objectives.

LESSONS LEARNED & FUTURE ORIENTED STATEMENT

Looking forward, I expect these past and current experiences to help me stand out in my future endeavors, including my professional aspirations, goals, and other leadership roles. I hope that my new leadership roles will not only look good on a resume, but also to hopefully begin my career in marketing or communications-related. Aside from professional development, I think the lessons I have learned as PBL State President has motivated me to become more involved with the UW community and the organization at the local, state, and national levels. As an undergraduate, I did not participate actively with many extracurricular activities due to adaptation in college studies and work. However, my [recent] role has helped me seek additional opportunities offered by FBLA-PBL and the UW campus.
