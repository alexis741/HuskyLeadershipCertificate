Core Values

Honesty – I value honesty because it is a gateway to creating meaningful relationships. It connects me and others to interactions that are open and straight-forward, building trust while maintaining these relationships to the people I affiliate with. 

Resilience – Today, I am a bit more optimistic and fully capable of understanding other peoples' perspectives. I value resiliency because prior to today, I have faced several adversities in my life, and through personal perseverance, I urged myself to face my fears, stressors, and consequences.

Security – I value security because I believe everyone has a right to keep their personal thoughts, ideas, belongings, and actions as private as they’ve like. It is important to control these entities, but it’s also important to liberate them and not distance yourself with your peers with such priveleged information.

Curiosity – The imagination of an individual is one of the most riveting things in the universe. I value curiosity because the willingness to invest in new, daring adventures has influenced my opportunities to express new interests while mastering modern life skills along the way.

Happiness – In my opinion, the formula for happiness is: energy, balance, and equity. Happiness is something I deeply value because these elements outweigh any achievement, efficiency, and other experiences. My happiness guides me where I want to go, what I want to do, and how I want to go about my outcomes and consequences.

Leadership Identities

Assisting – Assisting to anything is the start to making any impactful contributions to any benefiting organization. Regardless if it's for work or projects, I love giving back, whether if it's big or small-scale contributions. Assisting helps discover myself in several dimensions while creating wonderful moments for others.

Learning – You live and you learn, am I right? If you want something to succeed, you first assess yourself and develop a meaningful plan to get to where your curious or intellectual mindsets takes you. At most times, it'll take you to amazing new things, but at other times, it will not. As we mature everyday, it is not important what we can remember and retain such content; what you took away from it is what's important, as well as how you will apply that information into the future.

Mentoring – Wisdom and strategic planning are the two skills you'll need to mentor today's youth. A path to success does not indeed go in a straight line, and it is up to me to prepare others for future goals and life's aspirations. Similarly to assisting, they will look back at the milestones they've overcame, they will thank us for our tremendous hard work, and that itself is a remarkable feeling for supporting our recipients.

Organizing – Nothing says more than being prepared and punctual than organization. It's a more than a soft skill; it's a lifestyle, and is used everywhere in life's waking moments. It's important to me because organization is broken down to little nitty-gritty details, ideas, and collectively affects the people that makes up the purpose, objectives, and activities of any society. 

My "Why"

Nelson Mandela once said: "Education is the most powerful weapon which we can use to change the world."

I do what I do because I enjoy expressing my personality, ideas, efforts, and sometimes recipes to myself, family, friends, and my communities. I am a first-generation university student, born to two immigrant parents (now U.S. citizens). I've been a victim of several adversities throughout my life, but that doesn't stop me from being the best version of myself. My life's experiences so far have provided me the space to grow and continuously reflect as a person, and I've always wanted to thank the global community for making me the person I am today. Ultimately, I want to make a lasting impact that will forever change mankind, whether if it's in the communications or marketing industries, volunteering in community settings, or even influencing a person's life. Even when my days are busy, they are focused one at a time; otherwise, I balance the rest of my day with personal and/or professional interests. Never stop growing, because there's always something to learn from yourself and others around you everyday!
