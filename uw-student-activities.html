Student Activities Office

OVERVIEW

A key learning experience during my undergraduate education was working for the Husky Union Building (HUB) as a student office assistant (SOA) at the Student Activities Office (SAO). The HUB is one UW Seattle's centers for student life, serving those who are members of a registered student organization (RSO), including campus visitors and all other affiliates who utilize our resources, setup numerous events, meet with student government and HUB administrators, dine and shop.

My recent role as an SOA insisted responsibilities like a typical office assistant, but at the student level. This experience was valuable to me because it taught me about professionalism, ethical values, interactional and developing relationships with my coworkers, and the space to practice client service.

PERSONAL IMPORTANCE OF WORK

It took me nearly 3 years to become hired by the University of Washington since the start of my matriculation in Seattle. I've started working at SAO since October 2017. Over the past year, working as an SOA gave me the flexibility to work around my academic schedule, balancing my time and energy being proficient in my academic work while applying them in the office environment.

As the Student Office Assistant, I learned the value of time management, telecommunications, and professionalism. Previously, I've attained 2 internships that were not in an office setting, and they were not executed as I expected. However, my office experiences have led me to making connections with other HUB employees and administrators, while enriching the culture at SAO.

TASKS ACCOMPLISHED

  • Provided information and front desk services for over 900+ registered student organizations (RSOs)
  • Performed secretarial duties, such as taking client calls and distributing HUB mail
  • Checked-in with SAO advisers for appointments of individuals and groups
  • Represent the SAO email account by troubleshooting inquiries and technical issues
  • Communicated with several departments with the HUB, ASUW, and other UW entities​
  • Attended the Association of College Unions International (ACUI) 2018 conference

LEADERSHIP COMPETENCIES

  • Excellence: One of my exciting moments regarding SAO was representing them and the UW at the 2018 ACUI Conference. Along with several HUB staff and ASUW student leaders, we flew out to Anaheim, CA and I took away two things: how student union departments interconnect with each other as one, and a brief introduction about student union policies and legislation. Upon returning from the conference, I saw my position a little differently than what I used to. Working in the Student Activities Office gave me a new “lens” to see in the university community. However, ACUI has given me a broader scope of the roles of different student union departments, which was very useful applying such excellence at work because when a client or student arrives at SAO, I can direct them what they need to and where they need to go.

  • Ethics: In the workplace, I think about ethics all the time. Even during work, I often prepare myself for even the weirdest and toughest scenarios out there. As an RSO leader myself, I have to affiliate such campus activities with SAO, however, as an SAO student employee, there is a fine line between following the procedures and regulations of all RSOs, or to abuse my role for the benefit of the RSOs I affiliate with. After such training for my current role, ethics is personally reflected in the workplace. I have gained the knowledge of the office's purpose and functions, which also gave me an insight to their organizational methods, management information systems, and secretarial duties. Acquiring this knowledge from work, with the help of some academic courses, made me realize the importance of such privilege to the access of this information and related resources SAO offers. Not only did it made me to become ethical in the office, but to also increase my social responsibility and intellectual awareness of the public when situations with un-affiliated  or prodromal visitors arise in the HUB or near the office.
 
  • Productive Relationships: Cooperating with mostly full-time staff and advisors in an office setting was pretty new to me. It's important to be proactive while keeping a positive attitude because as you're communicating with co-workers, teamwork is necessary to get things done. At SAO, I am one of six student office assistants, and then there's our office intern, my office support supervisor, 6 advisers, and finally the Assistant Director for Student Activities. Despite our different roles, we all rely on each other to cooperate on all functions of SAO for clients that affiliate with UW registered student organizations and the ASUW. Esch shift on-the-job requires constant communication with everyone in the office, and the pace fluctuates every now and then. Simultaneously, all of us check up on each other almost daily, which is critical. Our office handles one of the largest number of RSOs in the west coast, and it's amazing how such a small department at the HUB is a vital function of student-led experiences at the University of Washington.
​​
  • Problem Solving: It's okay to always questions on-the-job. I certainly was afraid of asking them because it would make me seem "too new" for my role. I'm stationed at the front desk at SAO, with my typical shift consisting checking mail and e-mail, directing visitors and callers to relevant resources. However, my first quarter was a little stressful on-the-job because I not only had to manage my time between SAO and my academic workload, but to also learn how to quickly and efficiently solve problems on my own. There would be times where the office pace is slow and I'd work on some office projects given from other advisers, but at other times, I process such inquiries and requests one-a-at-time in order to prevent stressing out and attempting to multitask when I really shouldn't. If it wasn't for my office supervisor, I probably wouldn't taken into account the tips and tricks to become successful in my role, especially when there are moments when most of the staff (and sometimes my supervisor) is out of the office.

LESSONS LEARNED & FUTURE ORIENTED STATEMENT

Looking forward, I expect these past and current experiences to help me stand out in my professional aspirations, including my current affiliation with the Husky Union Building. I hope that my new leadership roles will expand my career options as I understand more on the importance of student unions and their role in and out of the UW. Aside from professional development, I think the lessons I have learned as an SOA have impacted my viewpoint of the HUB, and how I slowly began to enjoy working for students and their RSOs. As a member of several RSOs myself, it is important that there's always a purpose why many of our student union departments exist in the first place: to enhance the university community by providing a dynamic center while fostering student engagement.
